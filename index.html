My Story

The lap that changed everything

"Even if you didn't win, the important part is the experience you gained on track and what you have to work on for the future."

Karting started as a fun day out with my dad, but it quickly became something I cared about deeply. I only began competing seriously in August 2025 — and watching Formula 1 made me want to take the sport seriously and test what I could do under pressure.

One of my earliest memories is setting a lap time far slower than I wanted. Instead of feeling discouraged, I became more motivated to understand why and improve it. That mindset still drives me: every session is a chance to learn.

Off the track, I am organised, hard-working and friendly. I value being part of a team, learning from mistakes and improving one step at a time. Less than a year in, the progress I have made already feels incredible to me.

  • Patience and focus in high-pressure race situations
  • Confidence in traffic and cleaner overtaking decisions
  • Consistency over short sprints and long endurance stints

Driver Profile

How I race and what I am building

I race rental karts indoors and outdoors as an Advanced Race Academy driver, with most of my experience coming from TeamSport sessions and endurance events.

I learn quickly, adapt well in traffic and stay focused on cleaner overtaking and more consistent lap times.

Endurance racing suits me because it rewards concentration, communication, patience and smart decision-making just as much as speed.

Tracks and Experience

What each track taught me

TeamSport Reading

My main track is TeamSport Reading, where I regularly take part in Academy racing and members-only nights, including endurance races and Hot Pursuit sessions. This is where I build most of my pace, traffic awareness and race craft.

TeamSport High Wycombe

A physically demanding track that requires stamina and arm strength. It helps me improve control, endurance and focus under pressure.

TeamSport Farnborough

This track helps me adapt quickly to new layouts, braking references, traffic patterns and changing grip.

Silverstone

Silverstone is my favourite track because it is large, open and attracts strong drivers. I have taken part in sprint racing there, giving me valuable outdoor karting experience.
